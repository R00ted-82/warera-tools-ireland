📈 Daily Profit

What each product is worth per production point, and your projected daily profit — auto-filled from the game.

How to use

Type your War Era username and hit Load. You'll get your estimated profit per day (companies, salary, missions, case sales) and a table of which products pay best. It all fills in from the game automatically; then you can fine-tune:

  • Tick only the missions you actually complete.
  • Enter cases sold today, and any company moves / production changes.
  • Adjust the engine / staff boxes to try "what if". Totals update instantly.

Projected income

Per day, at current market prices. Tweak the throughput to model hypotheticals — everything recalculates live.

Profit by product

Net / PP = profit per production point at the best region bonus. Ceiling / day = Net/PP × your total daily throughput − employee wages — a theoretical upper bound if every company's output went to this one product (not a realistic target). Actual / day = what your companies currently make of it.

How this works · methodology
  • Net / PP = (salePrice − Σ(recipe × rawPrice)) × (1 + bonus) ÷ productionPoints. Recipes & production points from gameConfig; prices from the WarEra market (warerastats); the production bonus from the same region/country model the Migration Advisor uses (best region shown).
  • Daily throughput ≈ your employees' output (production × energy × 0.24 × (1 + fidelity) PP/day each), your own self-work (production × entrepreneurship × 0.24, no fidelity/wage), plus each company's automated-engine production. It's an estimate — edit the field to match your real numbers.
  • Ceiling / day is a theoretical upper bound: 100% of your total throughput on one product, minus your employee wages (a fixed cost) — not a realistic target. Actual / day sums what your companies currently produce of each item at their own region's bonus.
  • Items not on the warerastats feed (e.g. wood, paper) are priced from the live market order book (tradingOrder.getTopOrders — midpoint of best bid/ask).
  • Irish-citizens only (?bypass=1 lifts it).
