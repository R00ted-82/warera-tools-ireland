How MUs are selected
  • Owner nationality. The MU's owner must currently be an Irish citizen. Owners who've migrated away get filtered out, even if they founded the MU.
  • MU country. When the MU exposes its own country (under countryId, country, nationalityId, or nationality), it must be set to Ireland. MUs without any country field skip this check rather than being dropped, so missing data doesn't wipe the list.
  • Member majority. At least half of the MU's members must be Irish. MUs with a mostly foreign roster are dropped even when the owner is local.
  • In the expanded members list, Irish citizens are marked 🇮🇪 and non-citizens are marked 🌍 and dimmed. The Irish stat on each card shows the ratio.

🤝 Buddy Finder

Find a buddy for the Irish buddy system, or join the waiting list.

What's the buddy system?

Two Irish citizens hire each other at minimum wage. Net wages roughly cancel, but both companies gain a worker, so production goes up on both sides without a real wage bill. It only works if both sides have similar skill levels. An 80⚡ player paired with a 20⚡ player is a bad deal for the bigger producer. This tool helps you find a fair match.

Show me the maths · why buddy beats working for a stranger

The setup

You and Buddy each own a company. You both work 10 times a day. Two options:

  • Stranger: you work for someone else at 0.135/click. Your own company sits idle.
  • Buddy: you work at Buddy's for 0.092 (minimum wage). Buddy works at yours for 0.092.

What you and your company get per day

You earn (after 8% tax)+1.24+0.85
Your company pays out0−0.92
Your company producesnothinga day's work from Buddy
Coins in pocket+1.24−0.07

So you "lose" 0.07 coins. What do you get back?

A whole day of Buddy's work, done at your company. Roughly ~160 production points if Buddy's stats are decent. That's enough to make around 16 manufactured goods (concrete, food, weapons, whatever your company builds).

Selling those goods on the market brings in way more than the 0.07 coins you "lost". Exactly how much depends on your bonuses and current prices, but the gap is huge.

One bonus tip

Energy levels add +0.1 clicks/hour. Entrepreneurship levels add +0.05 clicks/hour. Energy is twice as good per skill point. So put your points into Energy and let someone else do the working at your company. The buddy system is how you do that.

Bottom line: you give up 0.07 coins a day. You gain a whole day of production. Both buddies win, as long as you're roughly the same skill level.

Numbers above are rough. The shape holds for any normal pair. If your pair becomes mismatched (this tool flags it), the bigger producer is losing out; switch to someone closer to your level.

How matching works · methodology

1. Your stats

We look up your ↗ production (PP per work click) and ⚡ energy (work clicks per day). Your max daily output is roughly production × energy × 0.343 (empirically calibrated from 10%/hour energy regen × ~7 energy per work action).

2. The candidate pool

Other Irish citizens who are either:

  • On the waiting list, prioritised
  • Currently unpaired, open to a new match
  • Mismatched, in a pair with a large skill gap, who could use a better partner

Players in balanced pairs are skipped, because pairing them would break a working arrangement.

3. Ranking

Three-tier sort: waitlist members first, then mismatched-pair members, then everyone else. Within each tier, closest skill level to yours wins.

The waiting list is self-cleaning

A daily job removes anyone who's since found a balanced buddy. People in mismatched pairs stay on, since they're getting shortchanged.

What if my skills change after I'm paired?

If a balanced pair drifts (one buddy grinds skills faster), the pair gets flagged as mismatched automatically, and both members become findable in match searches with an orange badge. You don't need to do anything. Just check back if you want a better match.

The data

Live calls to the War Era Gateway via the warera-proxy Cloudflare Worker. No data stored about you, except the username + userId on the waiting list (if you opt in). Everything else is computed fresh each time.

How this works
  • Production bonus has up to four components. Strategic resources and regional deposits are flat country/region facts; the two +30% bonuses are gated by the country's industrialism lean, a signed integer the game exposes via the warerastats data feed (range −2…+2 in current data, with 0 = neutral). The gating is asymmetric: industrialist-leaning (ind > 0) countries grant the +30 on their specialised item; agrarian-leaning (ind < 0) countries grant the +30 on matching regional deposits; neutral (ind = 0) countries grant neither. Each lean grants exactly one type of +30%, never both.
    • Strategic resources: the country's productionPercent, fires when the item matches the country's specializedItem. Independent of industrialism.
    • +30% Industrialism (specialisation): fires alongside strategic resources only when the country is industrialist-leaning AND specialises in the item.
    • Regional deposit (the deposit's bonusPercent, typically +30%): fires when the country does NOT specialise in this item AND the region has an active matching deposit.
    • +30% Industrialism (deposit): fires alongside a regional deposit only when the country is agrarian-leaning.
  • Industrialism is read directly, not inferred. The signed integer comes from the warerastats companion endpoint. If warerastats is unreachable, the tool degrades gracefully: industrialism defaults to 0 for all countries.
  • Income tax is deducted from workers' wages, not from the owner's share of production. So whether tax directly affects you depends on whether you actually work in the company.
  • You work here (badge shown on the card): ranking uses your take-home, (1 + bonus%) × (1 − income tax%).
  • No badge: you own the company but don't work in it. Ranking uses raw output, 1 + bonus%.
  • The percentage on each verdict pill is a relative increase, not a percentage-point change.
  • Numbers shown are regional/country bonuses only. Company-level upgrades aren't location-dependent, so totals here may be lower than what the game shows on your company.
  • Not modelled: migration cost, active wars or sanctions, worker recruitment effects of high tax, market and sales taxes, or political shifts.

How this works

The short version

Every time one of your workers does a work cycle, you pay them a wage. That wage is timestamped and stored in the game's transaction history. We pull those timestamps for each of your workers, group them into clock-in episodes, and plot them on a 48-hour timeline.

What the colours mean

  • Active — clocked in within the last 24 hours. They're working.
  • Slowing — last clock-in was 24 to 48 hours ago. Worth a nudge.
  • Idle — no clock-ins in the last 48 hours. They've stopped showing up.

What the timeline shows

The line under each worker is the last 48 hours, with "now" on the right and 48h ago on the left. Each green dot is one clock-in episode. Hover a dot to see the exact time, production points earned, and ₿ paid.

Lots of dots bunched together means a busy worker. A gap on the right means they've been quiet recently. Click Show detail on a worker to see each episode listed individually with the rest periods between them.

Episodes vs. individual cycles

A worker can do several work cycles back-to-back in quick succession — the game settles these as separate wage transactions but they all happen at roughly the same moment. To keep the timeline readable, cycles that happen within 4 minutes of each other are grouped into a single episode. The displayed production points and ₿ are the combined totals for the episode, so nothing gets lost — it's just shown as one dot instead of a cluster of overlapping ones.

Projected payroll

The panel under the worker list answers: roughly how much will I pay out over the next few hours? Three figures:

  • Next 3h / Next 6h — based on your average payroll rate over the last 24 hours. Total ₿ paid in the last day, divided by 24, multiplied by the window. A simple "if the next few hours look like the average of yesterday".
  • Next 10h (if maxed) — assumes every worker has been idle long enough for their energy bar to fill completely, then comes back and burns through it all. Each worker's max energy ÷ 10 (the cost of one work action) gives their number of clicks. The 10-hour window matters because energy regenerates at 10% of max per hour, so 10 hours is exactly how long it takes for an empty bar to fill back up — being idle for longer doesn't accumulate more. This is the worst-case payroll if the whole roster cashed out today.

Each card also shows the actual ₿ paid in the matching window (last 3h, last 6h, last 10h) — useful for spotting whether you're currently in a busier or quieter stretch than the 24h average suggests.

Things to know

  • One worker, one employer. In War Era a worker can only hold one work contract at a time, so every wage they receive is from you — that's why the clock-ins shown are real and complete.
  • Dot density isn't effort. A high-wage worker does fewer (but bigger) cycles than a low-wage one. Two workers with very different dot counts can be putting in the same hours.
  • Empty timeline isn't a fired employee. If someone's on contract with you but hasn't clocked in for 48h, you'll still see their card — just with no dots. They're idle, not gone.
  • The last few minutes may be missing. The data is scraped from the game, so very fresh clock-ins might not appear until the next scrape.

Sharing a view

Once you've loaded your roster, the URL updates to include your username (e.g. #clockin?u=toie). Bookmark it or share it — opening that link will reload the same view straight away.

For the curious: where the data comes from

Under the hood, this calls a few endpoints on the War Era Gateway: search.searchAnything and user.getUserLite to find your account, company.getCompanies + worker.getWorkers for your roster, and transaction.getPaginatedTransactions (filtered to wage) for each worker's recent clock-ins. We also keep each worker's energy and production stats from the same user.getUserLite call (no extra requests) for the "if maxed" ceiling. Wage transactions are modelled as trades — the worker sells their labour (sellerId), you buy it (buyerId) — so we keep only the ones where the worker is the seller, AND you're the buyer, to filter out their own outgoing payroll and any work they did for other employers. The whole thing runs in your browser. Nothing is stored.

How this works
  • The wealth breakdown is public: user.getUserById returns stats.wealth = { companies, items, money, equipments, weapons, total } — the exact figures on the profile. The current value is read live when you look a player up.
  • All Irish citizens are snapshotted daily by a GitHub Action into a per-player history file. The chart buckets those daily snapshots by day, week or month in your browser; the live value is plotted as the final point. Periods with no snapshots show as real gaps in the line.
  • Only Irish citizens can be looked up. Append ?bypass=1 to the URL to lift that gate for admin or debugging.
