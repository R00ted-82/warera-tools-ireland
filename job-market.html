Open slots by wage (net, after tax)

Each bar = how many open job slots are posted at that net wage. The dashed lines mark the floor and median.

How to read this · methodology
  • Pulls every open offer from workOffer.getWorkOffersPaginated (through the proxy). Each carries a net wage (wageAfterTax), slots left/total (quantity/initialQuantity) and region.
  • Fill rate = filled slots ÷ posted slots across all open offers. High = workers are snapping jobs up (good time to lowball); low = lots of jobs sitting unfilled (workers are picky, a lowball will sit empty).
  • Wage floor = the lowest net wage anyone's currently posting. To be the cheapest option, post at or just above it — but it only fills if workers are actually taking low offers (see fill rate).
  • Snapshot only. "Ripe vs normal" is a heuristic from the current fill rate — there's no history yet, so treat the verdict as a rough read. (Tracking it over time would make ripeness exact.)
